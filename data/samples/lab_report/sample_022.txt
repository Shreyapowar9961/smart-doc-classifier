AIM / OBJECTIVE:
To verify and study verification of ohm's law and to understand the relationship between
voltage and current under controlled conditions.

THEORY / BACKGROUND:
Verification of Ohm's Law is a fundamental concept in Physics. According to the theoretical framework,
voltage is directly proportional to current
when resistance remains constant. This relationship can be expressed
mathematically and verified through controlled experimentation.

APPARATUS / MATERIALS REQUIRED:
1. Beaker (500mL)
2. Breadboard
3. IC 7408
4. Stopwatch
5. Pendulum bob

PROCEDURE:
1. Set up the apparatus as shown in the circuit/experimental diagram.
2. Ensure all connections are secure and the circuit is properly grounded.
3. Gradually vary voltage from minimum to maximum range.
4. For each setting, record the corresponding current.
5. Repeat each reading 3 times to minimize random errors.
6. Tabulate all observations carefully.
7. Plot the graph of voltage vs current.
