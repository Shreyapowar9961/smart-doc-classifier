AIM / OBJECTIVE:
To verify and study simple harmonic motion analysis and to understand the relationship between
oscillation and frequency under controlled conditions.

THEORY / BACKGROUND:
Simple Harmonic Motion Analysis is a fundamental concept in Physics. According to the theoretical framework,
oscillation is directly proportional to frequency
when amplitude remains constant. This relationship can be expressed
mathematically and verified through controlled experimentation.

APPARATUS / MATERIALS REQUIRED:
1. Voltmeter
2. Breadboard
3. Resistors (various values)
4. Function generator
5. Conical flask

PROCEDURE:
1. Set up the apparatus as shown in the circuit/experimental diagram.
2. Ensure all connections are secure and the circuit is properly grounded.
3. Gradually vary oscillation from minimum to maximum range.
4. For each setting, record the corresponding frequency.
5. Repeat each reading 4 times to minimize random errors.
6. Tabulate all observations carefully.
7. Plot the graph of oscillation vs frequency.
